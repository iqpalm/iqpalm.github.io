Motivation for change

I have been working as a finance contractor for a number of years in different sectors.

There were many reasons for attending the bootcamp and changing careers, some of these are listed below.

University

I have always been interested in IT from my A-level days.

During universtity I undertook a combined honours degree split equally between Business and Computer Science

The Computer Science part of my degree gave me an understanding of programming languages such as Ada

However, for reasons unknown to me I decided to follow the business element of my degree into Finance

The interest in IT however has always been there and I am eager to follow this and develop this interest into a career hence the enrolment on to the bootcamp

Change in career

I have been working as a contractor in various sectors, this has enabled me to experience different company cultures and working practices.

The roles that I have undertaken have been dealing with data and taking this raw data and developing insights and reports for end users.

I have been thinking for a while that I would like to further develop my skills and transition away from Finance but still help users achieve their requirements

Lockdown experience

Lockdown provided me with the opportuntity to test if I still enjoyed problem solving using code.

I enrolled with Datacamp and started on their Python course

I enjoyed learning something new and I was surprised how quickly I picked up the fundamentals once I got going

Conversations with my brother

My brother is a developer with the civil service and I had numerous conversations with him about changing careers

He himself had changed careers be it through taking a IT masters but he explained that there was always something to learn and that no-one knows everything.

I think it was this statement that made me finally decide to take the plunge and attend the bootcamp...as there is always something to learn:)
