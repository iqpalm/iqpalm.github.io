Bootcamp Highlights

I have enjoyed the 14 weeks that I attended the bootcamp learning concepts from backend through to frontend

It definately reinforced my thinking that this was the right change for me

The start of the journey

Before the course started I was required to pass an entry challenge which was a selection of tasks set by Northcoders

In order to prepare for this challenge Northcoders advised to work through some pre courses which started with Javascript on freecodecamp

I was sceptical as I have always learnt via the traditional route of classroom participation. However, I put these doubts to one side and started to work through the course on freecodecamp.

I quickly realised that I was learning Javascript and the doubts that I had at the start were unfounded.

Finally, to properly prepare for the kind of tasks that would be in the entry challenge I subscribed to CodeWars and worked through their Javascript katas

This gave me an opportunity to implement what I had learnt in freecodecamp and design solutions

I attended the entry challenge and was accepted on to the bootcamp with the tutor praising me for my knowledge learnt up to that point

Backend

Introduction to express and understanding that particular functions can be invoked when a particular HTTP method and route combination are chosen.

I quickly understood why express was the most popular backend framework

I enjoyed understanding how to setup PSQL databases and seed the database both for testing using JEST and also live development

The culmination of seeding the databases and combining PSQL with Javascript and express to then develop API endpoints gave me a great deal of confidence and achievement

Frontend

I was fascinated in learning about the DOM and how to access it e.g. document.getElementById(/*"someId"*/);

Learning about React, components, props, hooks and useState

Going through the planning process of an app using Figma and understanding what is a component and where to position different states

A good rule of thumb to follow here is to place your state as low as possible, but as high as necessary.

Understanding useEffect and how it is used to fetch data from an API and render this data on to the app

Project - chariT

Enjoyed developing a new app in 8 days using new technology AWS, MySQL and React Native

Use of morning standups and kanban board helped to keep track of project and where resources were required

Enjoyed project pitching and the feedback that was given by tutors. My idea was a scavenger hunt game that could be played virtually

However, it was agreed to develop an app to match volunteers and organisations called chariT
